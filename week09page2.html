Spanish Phrases When Eating Out

Me gustaría pedir la sopa.- I would like to order the soup.

Por favor, me trae la carta.- Please bring me the menu.

Soy vegetariano/a.- I am vegetarian.

¿Contiene gluten?- Does it contain gluten?

Me puede traer otro?- Could you bring me another?

Podría llenar mi vaso?- Could I have a refill?

¿Puedo pagar con tarjeta?- Can I pay with card?

¿Puedo traerme una caja para llevar por favor?- Can you bring me a to-go box please?

Hay algo en mi comida.- There is something in my food.

La comida está muy rica.- The food is really good.

A Simple Story in Spanish

Cuando era niño, hace muchísimos años, mi familia y yo vivíamos en un pueblito desconocido. En el pueblo, que es mejor no decir su nombre, no había más de diez familias. Mucha de la gente eran ancianos, incluyendo a los niños. La iglesia era el único edificio en el pueblito dónde nunca la gente podía entrar. Cuando un viajero tenía mala suerte al encontrar nuestro poblado, él no podía encontrar comida ni agua. Durante la noche, la iglesia llamaba al extranjero y el extranjero trataba de entrar el edificio. Siempre era un juego para todos los ciudadanos, la persecución y la caza del viajero.

Ahora la población no existe, porque el sol es más brillante. Solamente yo, el niño anciano que vive cerca de la iglesia sin entrar. No tengo familia ni viajeros, solamente el aire fresco de las montañas.

Spanish in the US

Spanish in the US has boomed! With a rising Hispanic population, it's now the second most spoken language in the country. Communities have sprouted, bringing cultural and linguistic diversity. Schools are offering Spanish immersion programs and classes, promoting bilingualism. ¡Ole!

But it's not just about language, it's also about business. Companies realize the importance of catering to Spanish-speaking customers, creating Spanish services. And the connections with Spanish-speaking countries? They're blossoming too, bringing opportunities in translation, interpretation, and bilingual marketing. Spanish is building bridges and adding a spicy touch to American life. ¡Viva el español!

Why Learn Spanish?

Why should you learn Spanish? Well, let me break it down for you. Firstly, Spanish is one of the most widely spoken languages globally, opening doors to communicate with millions of people around the world. Imagine the connections and friendships you can build!

But it's not just about communication, amigo. Learning Spanish can also enhance your travel experiences. From exploring the lively streets of Mexico City to savoring delectable cuisine in Buenos Aires, speaking Spanish allows you to fully immerse yourself in the local culture and create unforgettable memories.

Furthermore, being bilingual in Spanish can boost your career prospects. With the increasing importance of global markets, many companies seek individuals who can confidently navigate and communicate in Spanish-speaking regions. So, don't miss out on the chance to expand your horizons and reap the benefits of speaking Spanish!
